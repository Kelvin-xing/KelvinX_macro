\Section{Final Producer}

Final producer solves
\begin{aligned}
	\max_{y_{jt}} P_t Y_t - \integral_0^1 p_{jt} y_{jt} dj\\
	\text{subject to} ln{Y_t} = \integral_0^1 ln y_{jt} dj
\end{aligned}

The marginal profit of the Final producer should equal the marginal cost:
\begin{aligned}
	\frac{\partial P_t Y_t}{\partial y_{jt}} = p_{jt}\\
	\Rightarrow
	P_t \times exp(\integral_0^1 ln y_{jt} d) \times \frac{1}{y_{jt}} = p_{jt}\\
	\Rightarrow
	P_t Y_t = p_{jt}y_{jt}
	\Leftrightarrow
	lnY_t &= \integral^1_0 ln \frac{P_tY_t}{p_{jt}}dj\\
		&= lnP_tY_t - \integral^1_0 ln p_{jt}dj
	\Leftrightarrow
	lnP_t = \integral^1_0 ln p_{jt}dj
\end{aligned}
which is the optimal demand.
